How I do it

  • Discovery
  • Visual Design
  • Development
  • Launch
  • Promote

To get started, we'll meet for a consultation to assess your needs. Once we have our initial direction, we'll review the audience and user base. Then it's research, research, research. We'll need to establish the content before we go any further. During this stage, I'll also set up all the technical stuff. Then it's on to creating a style guide and we finish off with our strategic direction review.

Next, it's time to develop a concept to move forward on. With input from both parties, I'll design a low fidelity wireframe that encompasses basic layout, content structure, and visual flow. Then, we'll develop the visual style by encompassing elements from our style guide. Then, we'll fine tune our visual style for interaction. This includes buttons, links, and calls-to-action. I'll initiate some basic user testing as well to iron out any unsure elements.

Actual development takes the longest as I use code to bring your project to life. I'll start out with the code boilerplate and framework to lay a foundation. If the design incorporates a CMS, it will be installed. If a base theme is being used or modified, it will be implemented. If the design will be completely custom, I'll use HTML, CSS, JS, and PHP to develop it. I'll constantly review and improve my work. I don't cut corners. More user testing will be completed at this stage as well.

One development is complete, final testing will be initiated on the production-ready website. This will take place in a development environment. Together, we'll go through the final review and develop a release plan. Any training will be completed before launch. After much hard work, we can finally transfer all the files and rights and launch your website!

It doesn't stop there. A website is never truly done. Upon launch, I'll ensure that all SEO requirements are met and any marketing strategies are implemented. I'll also track and handle any usability issues and bugs. Analytics will be set up, monitored, and aggregated so that any issues arising will be dealt with. I'll write a case study for the Success Evaluation so we can see just how effective the new website is.

Throughout my life, this fantastic duality has persisted between technology and nature. As a kid, I was tinkering with electronics one day and running through the forest the next. I was so curious and wanted to understand how everything worked. I started to obsess with the design of things. I'd constantly sketch and brainstorm ideas for improvements. I sought harmony and simplicity, so I combined principles of nature with design elements. I then went on to build my first computer at age 13, quickly delving into the inner workings. I fell in love with the digital world.

I dabbled in programming early on, albeit with only slight interest. My first introduction to HTML was about 8 years ago customizing MySpace profiles for friends. The direct visual output quickly fueled my excitement. So I got serious, ditched the bad habits, and really learned HTML and CSS. I built my first websites for friends and it quickly became a full-time passion. Now, I'm turning that into a profession. With that same child-like sense of wonder, I seek to understand and make the web a better, more beautiful place. I'm having an absolute blast along the way as I establish my venture.

Since freelancing allows me to set my own schedule, I make sure my days are well balanced. I'm still that same kid running through the forest so my days off are pretty fun. These days, I'm generally either hiking or climbing in the surrounding areas. I'll also take several backpacking trips in a year, usually at a week each with one long-distance trip. In the winter, things really pick up as I take up snowboarding and mountaineering. Rainy days usually send me to the garage to work on restoring my 1970 Honda CB350.
